**Step by step**

I cut down the black socks because they were too long.

![](https://bloghalfpintmama.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/img_1856-2-1.jpg?w=595)

I then stuffed the socks with newspaper, but you could really use anything. Tissue paper would work too.

![](https://bloghalfpintmama.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/img_1857-1.jpg?w=596)

 I closed that together with some black embroidery thread and a needle but again you could probably use anything. You could probably hot glue it if you wanted to.

Then I attached the sock legs to the onesie, two on each side of the torso.

![](https://bloghalfpintmama.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/img_1858-1.jpg?w=606)

They were very floppy after this, so I added embroidery thread to connect the sleeve to the sock and then the sock to the second sock. I actually think I‚Äôm gonna go in and do this again a little further down on the limbs to help give it a little more stabilization.

![](https://bloghalfpintmama.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/img_1859-1.jpg?w=874)

Finishing everything off with a bow that has spiderwebs on it. If I was creating a boy version of this, I would add a black beanie with some glued on googly eyes, but I wanted to make it cutesy for my little girl.
